# Tidewake — Vision

> One small boat. One open horizon. A legend you write yourself.

Tidewake is an experimental, open **3D age-of-sail pirate adventure** that runs in any
browser. It is original work — its world, characters, and humour belong to it alone, and
it never imitates or references any existing commercial game or franchise.

## The fantasy

You arrive in a sun-warmed archipelago with almost nothing: a tired little sloop, a few
coins, and a name no one has heard yet. The sea is **believable** — wind you can feel in
the sails, swell that lifts the hull, light that turns gold at dusk — but the people in it
are **warm, witty, and a touch ridiculous**. A harbourmaster who takes his ducks very
seriously. A merchant who swears every barrel is "artisanal." A rival captain whose threats
are mostly adjectives. The world is grounded; the company is good fun.

From that single boat, **you rise — by your own choices** — toward one of two legends, or
some tangled blend of both.

## The two paths

**The Pirate path — be FEARED.**
Raid merchant convoys, outrun the navy, bury and chase treasure, upgrade to ships that make
harbours go quiet when your sails crest the horizon. Reputation here is *dread*: bounties,
infamy, a flag people recognise. Ports may bar you, smugglers may court you, and the law
will eventually come looking. High risk, high romance, fast money, short memories.

**The Governance path — be RESPECTED.**
Buy a warehouse, then a wharf, then a stake in a sleepy port. Fund repairs, settle disputes,
fight off the *other* pirates, and grow a town that thrives because you steered it. Reputation
here is *standing*: titles, influence, a council that listens, streets that prosper. Slower,
steadier, and quietly powerful — the legend of the captain who put down the cutlass and picked
up the ledger.

**How they diverge and interweave.** The paths are not walls; they are a tide. Plunder funds
investment; investment buys protection; protection invites raids. A feared pirate can *buy
legitimacy* and become a governor with a colourful past. A respectable mayor can *quietly
fund* the very raiders that keep rivals weak. Your choices shift two reputations — **Infamy**
and **Standing** — and the world reacts: who greets you, who shoots first, what doors open.
There is no "right" ending, only the legend you actually earn.

## Core loops

The minute-to-minute and the hour-to-hour wrap around each other:

1. **Sail** — read the wind, trim the sails, ride the swell toward a chosen heading. Sailing
   itself is the toy: satisfying on its own before any goal.
2. **Explore** — find islands, ports, wrecks, rumours, and weather. The horizon always
   promises one more thing worth steering toward.
3. **Trade or Fight** — buy low and sell high across ports, or take what you want by force.
   The choice is yours every time, and it has consequences.
4. **Grow reputation** — every act nudges Infamy and/or Standing, unlocking ships, allies,
   contracts, ports, and threats.
5. **Invest or Conquer** — sink your gains into a bigger ship and bolder raids (Pirate), or
   into property, people, and a port you can govern (Governance) — then sail back out richer,
   stronger, and more notorious or more respected.

Each loop is short enough to enjoy in a single session and open enough to chain into an
evening-long climb.

## Pillars

- **The sea is the star.** If sailing isn't fun with nothing else on screen, nothing else
  matters. Wind, swell, and light come first.
- **Realism you feel, comedy you meet.** Atmospheric, plausible seafaring; expressive,
  exaggerated, genuinely funny characters and writing. Grounded world, light heart.
- **Your legend, your way.** Open-ended. The game offers paths and pressures, never a
  mandatory plot. Both Pirate and Governance must feel like a *complete* way to play.
- **Always playable.** Built in tiny, shippable increments; every release is a real,
  play-tested game, never a broken work-in-progress.
- **Original and open.** Open source, original IP, no franchise references — a clean world
  we can grow without borrowing anyone's.

## What makes it fun

The **joy of motion** — a small boat finding speed off the wind, heeling into a turn, the
camera swinging low over the swell. The **pull of the horizon** — there is always one more
island, port, or rumour just out of reach. The **weight of choice** — trade or raid, build
or burn, and a world that remembers which you chose.

## What makes it funny

The humour is **adventure-game humour**: character-driven, affectionate, and quotable. It
lives in port banter, item descriptions, loading-screen tips, signage, and the absurd dignity
of NPCs who take small things very seriously. The sea is played straight so the **people** can
be silly — a tonal contrast that lets a sincere storm and a ludicrous tavern argument share
the same world without breaking it. Fun first, always.
