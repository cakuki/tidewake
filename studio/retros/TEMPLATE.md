# Retrospective — Loop <N> (or Loops <N–M>)

_Date: YYYY-MM-DD · Facilitator: Project Manager · Present: all roles_

Held every 3–4 loops. Blameless: improve the system, not the person. Copy this file to
`retros/loop-<N>.md` and fill it in. Action items must name an owner **and** the exact
agent definition / runbook / file to update — otherwise nothing changes.

> **Every retro reviews two things, not one:** **(a) the product/game** and **(b) the
> studio itself — its workflow, collaboration, tooling, and process.** The explicit goal is
> to **optimise our workflows so we produce better, MORE CREATIVE results** — higher
> throughput *and* higher creative quality. Process improvements are first-class outcomes:
> retro action-items frequently edit `docs/runbook/LOOP.md` and the `studio/agents/<role>.md`
> definitions. **The process improves itself** — that's the point.

## What we shipped
- Releases, features, and player-facing changes since the last retro.

## Product / game review
- Did the slices move the north-star fantasy? Tone, fun, feel, polish — what landed, what missed.
- Where could the *result* be more creative, surprising, or charming next block?

## Studio / process review
- **Workflow & flow:** where did the process slow us, add rework, or create thrash?
- **Collaboration & communication:** hand-offs, inboxes, board honesty, role seams — what dulled momentum?
- **Tooling:** CI, the playtest gate, capture/video, scripts — what was friction vs. leverage?
- **Creativity:** where did the *process itself* dull creativity (rushed design, no room to explore,
  muddy feedback)? What workflow/tooling/communication change would raise **both throughput AND
  creative quality**?
- **Self-improvement:** what will we change in the **runbook** or an **agent definition** as a result?

## What worked (keep)
- Practices, tools, and decisions to deliberately continue (product **and** process).

## What didn't (improve)
- Friction, rework, missed acceptance, off-tone work, or flow stalls — with the why
  (product **and** process).

## Experiments to try
- Small, reversible changes to test next block. One hypothesis each: "If we …, then …".
