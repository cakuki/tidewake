_Date: 2026-06-28 · Facilitator: Project Manager (acting PM + Tech Lead + Game Designer) · Present:
all roles (run as a subagent)_

Twelfth studio retro, fired by the HARD trigger (7 build cycles since Retro 11). Retro 10 *built* the
mode spine; Retro 11 *made the town matter*; **Retro 12 is the chapter where the reactive loop
CLOSED end-to-end, the second reputation pole finally grew a verb, the open sea started looking
back, and the save system was hardened — after we caught a real bug that had been silently wiping
player progress on every schema bump.** Blameless: improve the system, not the person.

## What we shipped

- **Loop 62 — #112 + #111 + #115 Rumours that pay off (CLOSES #111 + #115; #112 stays OPEN as the

  sea-green pin on the heading, cleared the instant it resolves. **#112 payoff:** arriving pays a
  deterministic coin bounty + sings a 'rumour' Ballad verse (#78); `resolvesAt` guards double-pay.
  Asset-light (2D-canvas marker + DOM), zero new draws. 563 tests (+18). (`…052005`)
- **Loop 63 — #105 "While you were ashore…" digest (CLOSES #105).** Makes the "world keeps living
  underneath" promise legible: snapshot the delta-able world-state when TOWN takes the screen, then on
  Set Sail compose a short in-character digest from the REAL deltas (pole flip ▸ tier climb ▸ a
  heading taken ▸ purse/hold change ▸ a deterministic ambient line) onto the HUD toast. New pure
  `src/systems/ashore-digest.js`. Did **not** build offscreen world-sim — surfaced only deltas the
  existing systems already move. 577 tests (+14). (`…053810`)
- **Loop 64 — #69 Per-town music identity (CLOSES #69; #94 phase 3).** Every harbour shared one

  `setTargetAtTime` glide — so the port already sounds like itself as it swells in on approach. New
  pure `src/town-theme.js`; **tempo stays fixed** (TL constraint — live tempo glitches the scheduler).
  583 tests (+6). (`…055104`)
- **Loop 65 — #104b Port-memory DEPTH: a port recalls your last deed BY NAME (save v10→v11).** #104
  remembered your *face*; #104b remembers what you *did* there. `deedPhrase(event)` maps a combat win
  / rumour payoff onto a terse port-framed memory; `recordDeed` banks it without a visit (reputation
  precedes you); `recallLine` leads with the named deed. Also laid the **"Your Harbour" seed**
  (`homePort` = most-visited past a threshold) that Loop 67 promoted into a verb. 595 tests (+16).
  (`…061042`)
- **Loop 66 — #125 Emergent at-sea encounter: a foundering ship, RESCUE vs PLUNDER (CLOSES #125).**
  Enriched SAILING — our most-used but least-reactive mode — by turning traversal into a
  story-generator. A seeded founderer occasionally heaves into view and presents a real moral choice:
  **rescue** her crew (Standing) or **plunder** the wreck (Infamy + coin), resolving to the right pole
  + a distinct Ballad verse, then despawning cleanly. New pure `src/systems/encounter.js` (cadence
  keyed to distance SAILED, deterministic + headless-safe, not wall-clock); reuses the hero hull for
  the wreck (~1 draw call, only when on-screen). 618 tests (+23). (`…063323`)
- **Loop 67 — #118 Your Harbour: CLAIM & GROW a home port (CLOSES #118; save v11→v12).** The
  governor pole's first reactive verb — the lawful half of the fantasy was still just a number while
  Infamy had raid/false-colours/legend. At sufficient Standing you **claim** your docked port, then
  **spend coin** to **grow** it a level at a time; each investment earns Standing and warms a
  homecoming greeting as the port prospers. New pure `src/systems/home-port.js` (claim/invest ladder,
  fail-open); a 🏠 Your Harbour town panel; a `harbour` Ballad deed type. 643 tests (+25). (`…065245`)
- **Loop 68 — #122 Declarative save-migration codec + frozen old-save corpus (CLOSES #122).** The
  process headline (see below). The save schema had been bumped v8→v12 in ~20 cycles via an
  ever-growing ad-hoc sanitise god-function, and `deserialize` HARD-rejected any `v !== SAVE_VERSION`
  → **every bump silently wiped player progress.** Replaced the gate with an ordered, declarative
  forward-migration pipeline (`migrations[v] = fn`, v_n → v_{n+1}) feeding the single validated reader
  (fail-open preserved as the final net), a pure total `migrate(obj)`, and a **frozen old-save
  corpus** (one real blob per historical version v1..v11) asserted to migrate forward without loss —
  the gate that stops the next bump (battle #100) from eating a voyage. No live format change (still
  v12). 669 tests (+18). (`…070429`)

## Product / game review — what the build has BECOME

**Retro 11 made the town a destination; Retro 12 closed the loop around it and made the world
symmetric.** Four things changed the shape of the game in seven cycles:

> **The reactive loop is now CLOSED end-to-end.** You walk into a tavern, listen for word, get a
> rumour that names a real target, set a **marker** for its heading, sail there, and **arrive to a
> reward** — coin in the palm and a verse in the Ballad — and on Set Sail the watch **reads your visit
> back to you**. Town → rumour → sail → payoff → consequence is a full circuit now, not a pointer that
> goes nowhere. **Both reputation poles finally have symmetric verbs.** The pirate half always had
> raid / false-colours / legend; the governor half was just a number on the needle. Now you can
> **claim a home port and raise it with your own coin** (#118), earning Standing and a homecoming that
> warms as the place prospers — the lawful captain's answer to plunder: *you don't take a place, you
> raise one.* **The open sea looks back at you** — a foundering ship is a mirror (#125): rescue and
> sail off respected, plunder and sail off richer and feared. And **each port now sounds like itself**
> (#69), so the world has a voice as well as a memory.

This chapter delivered the two cross-cutting things DL #4 asked for: **close the reactive loop across
every discipline** (dev #115 + the marker/payoff, audio #69, legibility #105) and **give the unbuilt
pole a verb** (#118). The build is no longer a half-symmetric fantasy with one rich pole and one inert
one — both the pirate and the governor paths now have a first-class reactive verb, and the most-used
mode (sailing) is no longer the least reactive.

**The one honest gap remains the same one held the last two retros: battle is still an empty room.**
That is deliberate — #100 has been held for the owner's Game-Designer brief, which has now been
*waiting the entire session with the owner absent.* It is the obvious next big-leverage slice and the
single largest piece of unbuilt content, but it is the owner's to steer. **It may be worth a gentle
owner nudge** (see process findings) rather than letting it sit held indefinitely while smaller work
drains around it.

## Studio / process review

**What's reliable now (keep).**
- **Seven green slices, +127 honest TDD-first tests, 0 escaped bugs, clean trees throughout.** Every
  hardening discipline from Retro 5–11 (lean orchestrator, self-shipping runners, injection guardrail,
  clean-tree check, hard ritual trigger, phase-labelled umbrellas, QUEUE-SYNC) held through a heavy
  mixed block of features + a refactor.
- **The Retro 11 re-sort dispatched cleanly.** The top trio (#112/#111 → #105 → #69) shipped in
  *exactly* the predicted order; the DL #4 reservoir then drained in a sensible order
  (#104b → #125 → #118 → #122) without re-litigation. QUEUE-SYNC + PHASE-LABEL are doing their job.
- **Pole symmetry as a design check.** #118 was chosen because the *needle had a verb on only one
  side* — a useful standing lens: when one pole gets a mechanic, ask what the mirror is.

**What we learned / the findings.**
1. **HEADLINE — ad-hoc schema bumps were silently destroying saves; schema changes now need a
   migration + a corpus, ENFORCED.** This is the most important finding of the session. The save
   schema was bumped **v8→v12 in ~20 cycles** — each bump a small, reasonable-looking edit to a
   growing sanitise function — and `deserialize` had *always* hard-rejected any save whose version
   didn't equal the current one. Net effect: **every single schema bump silently wiped any existing
   player's progress on load.** It passed every gate because the tests only ever round-tripped a
   *current* save; nothing in the suite held an *old* save and asserted it survived. The fix (#122)
   was structural, not a patch: a declarative forward-migration pipeline + a **frozen old-save corpus
   (one real blob per historical version) with a coverage guard that fails the build if a prior
   version lacks a frozen blob.** **The lesson, now a standing rule:** *a save-schema change is not
   done until (a) it migrates every prior version forward and (b) a frozen blob of the new version is
   added to the corpus.* The "fail-open + sanitise" pattern we'd been congratulating ourselves on
   since Retro 11 was real, but it was fail-open on *field* sanity — it never protected against the
   *version gate* throwing the whole save away first. A pattern that looks like discipline can still
   hide a silent data-loss bug one layer up; only an old-data corpus proves it.
2. **A from-owner OWNER-DECISION held across an entire session needs an escalation, not just patience.**
   #100 battle has been correctly *not* auto-promoted (it is the owner's call to steer) — but it has
   now sat `[OWNER-DECISION]` since before Retro 11, through the whole of this session, with the owner
   absent and the brief unattended. **Finding:** "do not auto-promote" and "let it sit silently
   forever" are not the same call. A held owner-decision that blocks the single biggest slice on the
   board should, after ~one retro cycle of silence, trigger a **gentle owner nudge** over the channel
   (with options, never auto-adopting) — keep it held, but make sure the owner *knows* it's the thing
   waiting on them. Logged as an action item; it stays HELD.
3. **Gate-hardening continues to earn its keep — extend it to data, not just state-space.** Retro 11's
   lesson was "schedule a QA-coverage slice each time the state space grows." #122 generalises it:
   the *data* the player accumulates (the save) is a state space too, and it had been growing
   unguarded. The next hardening slices (#123 golden-replay, #121 gate invariants) should ride with
   battle #100 for the same reason the save corpus rode ahead of it: **harden the thing battle will
   stress before battle stresses it.**

**Creativity (process self-review).** The worry going in was that a refactor/hardening slice (#122)
and an audio slice (#69) would read as low-charm filler next to the headline verbs. They didn't —
#122 carried a genuine creative spark (*"an old save is a message in a bottle; you teach the ship to
read the older hands"*) and #69 gave the world a literal voice. The real risk *ahead* is the
opposite: with the reactive loop closed and both poles verbed, the temptation is to keep adding
*small* reactive flourishes (more encounter types, more digest lines, more port charm) and keep
deferring the **empty battle room**, which is now both the biggest content gap and a held owner
decision. Hold depth-over-breadth, but don't let "polish the closed loop" become a way to avoid the
room nobody has opened.
