# Retrospective — Loops 55–61 (Retro 11)

_Date: 2026-06-28 · Facilitator: Project Manager · Present: all roles (run as a subagent)_

Eleventh studio retro, fired by the HARD trigger (7 build cycles since Retro 10). This is **the
"make the modes MATTER + world breadth" chapter**: where Retro 10 *built* the mode spine (a
sailing/town/battle state machine, a real town/market mode, a mode-aware sound bed, a hardened
seam), Retro 11 covers the block that gave that skeleton a **reason to exist**. In seven cycles the
town stopped being a better trade panel and became a **destination with a memory and reactive
rumours**, landfall stopped being a snap and became a **crafted multi-sensory moment**, the world
between ports filled out with **varied isles and a living sea**, and the gate caught up to the
exploding state space (plus a **real headless perf race**). Blameless: improve the system, not the
person.

## Product / game review — what the build has BECOME

**Retro 10 built the rooms; Retro 11 gave them a reason to walk into.** The honest read at the last
retro was "the spine is a skeleton, not yet a body — town is closer to a better trade panel than a
destination, battle is an empty room." Seven cycles later:

> **Town is now a destination, not a menu.** You sail back to a port because it *holds something for
> you*: a tavern regular who "heard a thing" hands you a **rumour that names a real target** — a soft
> objective you chase with the verbs you already have — and the harbourmaster **remembers your face**,
> greeting a returning regular differently from a stranger, warmer or cooler for who you've become
> since you last tied up. The **arrival itself is now a crafted moment** — the helm goes quiet, the
> ship glides to her moorings, the sea eases to glass, the light turns gold and a bright chord rings
> out on the beat — *you've arrived* — instead of a screen snapping over. And the canvas between ports
> is alive: **every isle wears its own face** (palette, silhouette, palms and rocks and driftwood) and
> the **sunny sea drifts with whitecaps** that froth on the crests and break apart on the swell.

This is **depth and breadth in the same chapter, both done right.** The depth half (rumours →
memory → crafted landfall) closes the loop DL #3 asked for: a *reactive verb ashore* (#103), a *town
that remembers* (#104), and a *transition that is the drama* (#102). The breadth half (#71 isles,
#70 whitecaps) widened the world without adding a single noun the player has to manage — pure
deterministic character on existing geometry, instanced and shader-only so the perf budget never
moved. And the gate kept pace: #107 turned the exploding mode state-space into a committed N×N matrix
+ golden trace, and in doing so flushed out a **real** perf-counter race that the happy-path gate had
been masking.

**The one honest gap that remains is the same one held last retro: battle is still an empty room.**
The town now *matters*; the third mode does not yet exist as content. That is deliberate — #100
battle has been held for the owner's Game-Designer brief — and it is the obvious next leverage.
**The rumour verb also doesn't yet pay off:** #103 points the bow somewhere worth going, but arriving
there has no reward and the heading has no marker (#111/#112 filed). Closing that reactive loop —
town → rumour → sail there → *payoff* — is the cheapest high-value depth slice on the board.

## Studio / process review

**What's reliable now (keep).**
- **Seven green slices, +60 honest TDD-first tests, 0 escaped bugs, clean trees throughout.** The
  Retro 5–10 hardening (lean orchestrator, self-shipping runners, injection guardrail, clean-tree
  check, hard ritual trigger, phase-labelled umbrellas) all held through a mixed depth+breadth block.
- **The Retro 10 re-sort dispatched cleanly all block.** The breadth-pivot top-3 it set
  (#104 → #71 → #70) shipped in *exactly* the predicted order, and the depth trio before it
  (#103 → #102 → #107) ran off the prior re-sort without re-litigation. QUEUE-SYNC + PHASE-LABEL are
  doing their job — a cold resume read a true top line every cycle.
- **Save-migration discipline is a standing pattern now.** #104's v8→v9 bump used the same
  fail-open + sanitise + size-cap shape as legends/onboarding/ballad before it. Schema growth is
  routine, not risky.

**What we learned / the findings.**
1. **Gate-hardening slices earn their keep by catching *real* bugs, not just adding tests.** #107 was
   filed as "insurance as the state space explodes" — and it immediately surfaced a **genuine
   headless perf-counter race** (reproduced 1/6 runs) that the happy-path gate had silently tolerated.
   **Finding (keep):** continue scheduling a QA-coverage slice each time the mode/state space grows
   (battle #100 will warrant the next one); treat an intermittent gate result as a *bug to root-cause*,
   never as environmental noise.
2. **A from-owner umbrella that hosts a *standing rule* should stay OPEN deliberately — and the queue
   must say why.** #70 shipped a real slice (whitecaps) but **stays OPEN on purpose**: it's the home
   of the standing "1–2 sea-delight beats per loop" rule *and* its literal first slice (sail-over
   curios) is still unbuilt. **Finding:** distinguish "OPEN, phase shipped" (PHASE-LABEL rule) from
   "OPEN, hosts an ongoing rule" so a future retro doesn't mistake #70 for closeable. Noted on the
   queue line.
3. **The reactive loop is half-built and the gap is now legible.** #103 shipped the verb but its
   payoff (#112) and legibility (#111 marker) were correctly deferred as follow-ups rather than
   bolted on. **Finding:** that was the right call (smallest always-working increment), but the loop
   should now *close* the verb before adding new ones — a verb with no payoff is the same trap as a
   town with no destination, one layer up.

**Creativity (process self-review).** This was the first block where depth and breadth ran in the
same chapter, and the worry — that breadth slices (#71/#70) would read as filler next to the
high-value town verbs — didn't materialise: both carried a named CREATIVE SPARK (the harbourmaster
who remembers your face; the trade winds catching the crests) and both held the perf budget by
construction. The risk *ahead*: with the town now genuinely a destination, the temptation is to keep
deepening town (more verbs, more memory) instead of either **closing the rumour loop** or facing the
**empty battle room**. Hold depth-over-breadth *and* finish-the-loop-before-opening-a-new-one.
