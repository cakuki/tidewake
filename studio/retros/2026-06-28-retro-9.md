# Retrospective — Loops 41–47 (Retro 9)

_Date: 2026-06-28 · Facilitator: Project Manager · Present: all roles (run as a subagent)_

Ninth studio retro, fired by the HARD trigger (7 cycles since Retro 8, the new relaxed cadence's
first full count). This block had two distinct halves. **First, the studio drained the Retro 8
roadmap to the letter** — deception-as-a-verb (#79 → #91), then the hero-ship art leap (#55 research
→ #32 glTF hull), plus feel + visual fixes (#20 eased steering, #65 hull bilge cap) and a combat
depth beat (#72). **Then the owner stopped the loop and filed a large feedback batch through a live
PM desk** (#66, #67, #93–#101) that redraws the roadmap: the next direction is no longer "more charm
on the sandbox" — it is **structure**. The owner wants **modes** (sailing / town / battle, the world
continuing underneath), a **real town/market mode**, **auto-harbouring**, and a **unified
mode-aware sound system**. Retro 9's central job is to catch the queue up to that pivot. Blameless:
improve the system, not the person.

## Product / game review

**The Retro 8 plan executed cleanly and the game got both deeper and better-looking.** Deception is
now a first-class verb on *both* poles — bluff your way in and betray for Infamy (#79), or hunt
outlaws honestly under a letter of marque for Standing (#91), with the bluff a real gamble at the top
of the Infamy ladder. That same block made the hero asset finally *look* the part: the textured
Kenney carrack under a hand-stitched black pennant is the most shareable the boat has ever been — and
it cut draw calls, buying perf headroom. #72 then turned the cannon exchange into a genuine choice
(drown for swagger vs. break nerve for mercy), wiring combat outcomes back into the two-pole identity
spine. Two quiet fixes mattered out of proportion to their size: #65 (no more "water in the boat")
removed an embarrassing tell from every screenshot, and #20 gave the helm weight. **Breadth stayed
~zero — correct.**

**But the leading edge has moved.** The owner's PM-desk batch is unambiguous about where value lives
next, and it is *structural*, not more sandbox charm:

> **Recommendation: build the MODE SYSTEM the owner asked for. Top-3: (1) #95 mode scaffold →
> (2) #67 + #96 auto-harbour into a real town/market mode → (3) #94 unified mode-aware sound.**
> Reasoning: the owner stopped the loop specifically to describe a game with *places and states*, not
> just an open sea — sailing that **pauses** while the world continues, harbours you actually **enter**
> (a market mode you **Leave** via a button), and a **battle** mode (held for an 08:00 Game-Designer
> owner brief, #100). #95 is the keystone enabler — a small mode state machine with sailing as the
> default and a clean transition seam — and it unblocks #96 (town), #67 (the approach that triggers
> the switch), and #100 (battle). #67 + #96 then make the first new mode real, reusing the #76c
> slow-to-stop as the approach easing and folding in the #66 mobile market-overlap bug (#96 already
> scopes that fix). #94 makes each mode *sound* distinct (sailing/town/battle tracks + a proximity
> "port nearby" crossfade cue, absorbing #69 per-town music). These are **depth** in the truest sense
> — they turn "an ocean you sail" into "a world with rooms" — and they are exactly what the owner
> directed, so per the constitution's from-owner-P1 rule they preempt everything previously queued.

The rest of the owner batch (#97 living fauna, #93 ship's-wheel touch steering, #101 CC0 props, #71
islands TLC, #70 ocean micro-details, #68 seagulls) is good charm/atmosphere that slots in as fillers
once the mode spine exists. The DL #2 reservoir (#80 juice, #81 creak, #82 crew chorus, #83
watercolour chart) and cannon depth (#72) remain fine between-mode fillers but are no longer the
leading edge.

## Studio / process review

**What's reliable now (keep).**
- **Seven green slices, zero escaped bugs, +63 honest TDD-first tests, clean trees throughout.** The
  Retro 5–8 hardening (lean orchestrator, self-shipping runners, injected-context guardrail, clean-tree
  check, hard ritual trigger) all held with no recurrence. The execution machine is mature.
- **The relaxed cadence (Retro 8) worked as intended** — 7 cycles of shipping before this retro, and
  this retro has a *substantive* delta (a whole roadmap pivot) rather than "nothing new since last
  block." Lifting the trigger 4 → 7 was the right call.
- **The PM desk caught a real owner pivot in depth** — the batch is well-triaged in the REGISTER
  (value notes, mode/town split into #95+#96, battle held for an owner brief, dedup done).

**What hurt / the findings.**
1. **The queue drifted hard out of sync with the owner's intent — the retro is the catch-up.** A live
   PM-desk session (during the owner-ordered loop stop) filed a major batch (#66/#67/#93–#101) that
   redraws the roadmap to a P1 *mode system*, but `queue.md` still recommended **#79/#55/#32 — all now
   shipped** — and carried *none* of the new from-owner P1s. The resume brain (`loop-state.md`) listed
   the new items as "file/queue once committed," but a flat to-file list is **not** a prioritised
   queue, and nothing re-sorted the queue top before the next dispatch would have read it. Had the loop
   resumed cold, the orchestrator would have read a stale #79-era top line. **Fix:** after any PM-desk
   intake batch, re-sorting `queue.md`'s top (not just appending to a to-file list) is a required step
   before the next build dispatch — added to `LOOP.md`. This retro performs that re-sort.
2. **A runner adopted another runner's uncommitted WIP (#32, Loop 46) — a soft clean-tree-rule case.**
   The Retro 6 clean-tree rule says "foreign uncommitted work → STOP & flag." Loop 46 instead *adopted
   and finished* a prior cycle-runner's in-progress glTF swap (and fixed two real gaps: a Node-breaking
   `three` import in the test, and a 404'ing external texture). Good outcome, but it blurs the rule.
   **Clarification (logged, no code change):** the clean-tree STOP targets *unrelated* foreign WIP;
   WIP that is plainly the **same in-flight slice** the runner is assigned may be adopted-and-finished,
   and the runner must say so in its report (Loop 46 did). The rule still bites for anything off-slice.
3. **A near-duplicate slipped in (#98 ≈ #93, ship's-wheel).** The async loop filed #98 for the same
   ship's-wheel idea the PM desk had filed as #93; #98 was closed as a dup. Already captured in memory
   ("PM desk: dedup before filing"). Reinforced here: check open `from-owner` issues before filing.

**Creativity (process self-review).** This block was a healthy mix — system depth (deception both
poles, capture-vs-sink morality), an art multiplier (the carrack), and feel (eased helm). The process
did not dull creativity. The risk *ahead* is the opposite: the mode-system work is structural and can
read as "plumbing," so each mode slice must still carry a CREATIVE SPARK (the town's first market
barker, the cannon-smoke transition into battle, the harbour bell on auto-dock) so structure ships
*with* charm, not after it.
